# White-Tailed Eagle Spatio-Temporal Analysis in Serbia

This repository presents a GIS-based spatio-temporal analysis of white-tailed eagle (Haliaeetus albicilla) observations in Serbia during the 2023–2025 period.

The project integrates biodiversity occurrence data, temporal GIS analysis, seasonal distribution patterns, and movement path analysis in order to explore spatial ecology and migration behavior of the species.

## Project Overview

The analysis was developed using occurrence records obtained from the Global Biodiversity Information Facility (GBIF) and processed within a GIS environment to identify:

- seasonal spatial distribution patterns,
- migration pathways,
- critical ecological zones,
- and temporal movement dynamics.

The study focused on understanding how seasonal changes influence spatial behavior and habitat use of white-tailed eagles across different regions of Serbia.

## Methodology

The workflow included:

- validation and filtering of occurrence records,
- temporal classification by season,
- spatial visualization of occurrence patterns,
- points-to-path movement analysis,
- and interpretation of seasonal migration dynamics.

## Spatial and Temporal Findings

The analysis identified several important spatial and temporal patterns:

- strong association with major river corridors such as the Danube and Sava,
- increased spring mobility linked to migration toward breeding areas,
- winter concentration near wetland and river ecosystems,
- and reduced summer spatial dynamics associated with nesting behavior.

The results also highlighted several ecologically important areas, including:

- Fruška Gora,
- the confluence of the Tisa and Danube rivers,
- and lowland river ecosystems of northern Serbia.
